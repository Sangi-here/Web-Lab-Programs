Festivals In India

Some major festivals in India are Holi,Diwali,Onam, etc.The festivals can be national or religious.Some national festivals are Independance Day,Republic Day,Gandhi Jayanthi, etc.The national festivals are celebrated together as a nation,

Diwali

Diwali, or Deepavali, is India's "Festival of Lights," celebrating the spiritual victory of light over darkness and good over evil. People thoroughly clean and decorate their homes with colorful rangoli, flower garlands, and, most importantly, rows of earthen oil lamps called diyas.

Holi

Holi, the "Festival of Colours," celebrates the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil through vibrant celebrations of throwing colourful powders and water, singing, dancing, and feasting.

Onam

Onam is a vibrant ten-day harvest festival in Kerala, India, celebrating the return of the benevolent King Mahabali to his people, marked by grand feasts called Onasadya, intricate flower carpet designs called Pookalam, traditional boat races known as Vallam Kali, colorful dances like Thiruvathira and Pulikali, and a spirit of unity, prosperity, and cultural pride for the Malayali community worldwide.
